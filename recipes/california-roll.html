California Roll

Description

The California Roll is a popular sushi roll known for its fresh, mild flavor and beginner-friendly ingredients. It typically features imitation crab meat (or real crab), creamy avocado, and crisp cucumber, all wrapped in seasoned rice and a sheet of nori (seaweed). Often rolled inside-out with the rice on the outside and sprinkled with sesame seeds or tobiko (fish roe), this roll offers a perfect balance of textures and flavors. It’s a great introduction to sushi for those new to it, and a favorite choice for casual sushi lovers everywhere.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sushi rice (cooked and seasoned with rice vinegar)
  • 1 sheet nori (seaweed)
  • 2–3 sticks imitation crab (or real crab meat)
  • 1/4 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cucumber, julienned (thin sticks)
  • Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)
  • Soy sauce (for dipping)
  • Pickled ginger (optional, for serving)
  • Wasabi (optional, for serving)

Steps

  1. Place a bamboo sushi mat on a flat surface and cover it with plastic wrap.
  2. Lay a sheet of nori, shiny side down, on the plastic-covered mat.
  3. Wet your fingers and spread a thin, even layer of sushi rice over the nori, covering the entire sheet.
  4. Sprinkle sesame seeds on top of the rice (optional), then flip the nori so the rice faces down.
  5. Place imitation crab, avocado slices, and cucumber sticks in a line along the center of the nori.
  6. Using the mat, gently roll the sushi away from you, pressing firmly to shape it into a tight roll.
  7. Remove the roll from the mat and use a sharp, wet knife to cut it into 6–8 equal pieces.
  8. Serve with soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi on the side.
